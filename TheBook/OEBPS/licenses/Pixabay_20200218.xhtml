At one point in time, Pixabay used the CC0 license for all images and videos uploaded to their site.

Once they had a rather significant number of images, they silently changed the usage license to their own. Bait and Switch cow manure.

The new license is only compatible with use in the CC BY-SA field guide if the following conditions are met:

  • Only still image photographic content is used.
  • No people are used in the image. This satisfies restriction c.
  • No products, brands, or trademarks are used in the image. This satisfies restriction d.
  • The image is modified in such a manner that it is readily visually different from the image as it appears on Pixabay. Cropping the image meets that requirement. This helps satisfy restriction a and restriction b.
  • The largest side of the image is not more than 2048 pixels and the smallest side of the image is not more than 1024 pixels. This helps satisfy restriction a and restriction b.

Use of Pixabay content is discouraged but Content Curators for this field guide are allowed to use images from Pixabay when suitable content is not available from a CC BY-SA, CC BY, CC0, or Verifiable Public Domain source as long as the above guidelines are met.

However Content Curators are encouraged to attempt contact the photographer to both get the real name of the photographer and explicit permission outside the context of Pixabay to use the image under the terms of the CC BY-SA license.

Glossary of Terms

Jump to letter: A C G I M P S

Allele
A specific variation of a gene that occurs at the same place on the chromosome as other variations of the same gene. New alleles are created by random mutations in the copying of existing alleles, and may rapidly spread by organism reproduction through the species via natural selection if the mutation provides a benefit to the species.
Cryptic Species Complex
A species complex for which it is very difficult or even impossible to classify some specimens to the specific species within the complex based purely on the visually expressed phenotype.
Genotype
The genetic makeup of a particular individual organism, the specific alleles that specific organism inherited from its ancestors.
Introgression
The transfer of alleles that evolved in one grouping of organisms into another grouping of organisms through hybridization events between the two groupings.
Monophyletic
When a grouping of organisms is descended from a most recent common ancestor to the exclusion of all organisms not within the grouping, the grouping is called a monophyletic grouping. Contrast with paraphyletic.
Paraphyletic
When a grouping of organisms for which known organisms outside the grouping also descended from the most recent common ancestor of the grouping, the grouping is called a paraphyletic group. In the taxonomy ‘Tree of Life’ (e.g. Class, Order, Family, Genus, Species, etc.), paraphyletic groups are avoided as much as possible. Contrast with monophyletic.
Phenotype
The observable expression of a genotype, influenced by natural selection or (in captivity) selective breeding. Please note that it is not always easy to determine if an observable characteristic is the result of the genotype or another factor, such as diet or climate.
Species Complex
Two or more species that are so closely related they are difficult to distinguish from each other and may still have frequently occurring gene flow (introgression) happening.

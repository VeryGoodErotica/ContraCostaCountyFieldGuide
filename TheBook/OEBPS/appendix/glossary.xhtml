Glossary of Terms

Jump to letter: A C E G I M N P Q S T U V

Allele
A specific variation of a gene that occurs at the same place on the chromosome as other variations of the same gene. New alleles are created by random mutations in the copying of existing alleles, and may rapidly spread by organism reproduction through the species via natural selection if the mutation provides a benefit to the species.
Chordate
Animals within the phylum Chordata. All vertebrates are members of the Chordata phylum.
Chronospecies
A species defined in the context of a time frame. Many chronospecies have evolved into populations with a distinctively identifiably different phenotype and are therefore classified as a different species in the newer time frame.
Conspecific
When two or more populations are the same biological species, they are said to be conspecific.
Cryptic Species Complex
A species complex for which it is very difficult or even impossible to classify some specimens to the specific species within the complex based purely on the visually expressed phenotype.
Extant
Continues to have a surviving population.
Extirpated
A formerly reproducing population that no longer exists is an extirpated population. Locally (or globally) extinct.
Genetic Diversity
The variation of alleles available within a population.
Genotype
The genetic makeup of a particular individual organism, the specific alleles that specific organism inherited from its ancestors.
Introgression
The transfer of alleles that evolved in one grouping of organisms into another grouping of organisms through hybridization events between the two groupings.
Monophyletic
When a grouping of organisms is descended from a most recent common ancestor to the exclusion of all organisms not within the grouping, the grouping is called a monophyletic grouping. Contrast with paraphyletic.
Monotypic
A taxonomical grouping containing only one species. A taxonomic grouping higher than the species level (such as a genus) that only contains a single species is referred to as a monotypic grouping, whether or not multiple subspecies are described. A species that does not contain any subspecies is less commonly but also referred to as a monotypic species.
Mutualism
Describes the ecology where two or more biological species interact with each other for the mutual benefit of all species involved.
Natural History
In the context of a species, the study of the natural behaviours of a species in all of its life stages and how the species interacts with the environment around it and the ecogical niche in ecology of the environment around it.
Neotonic
In biology, neotonic generally refers to an animal in the larval stage of a species that does not go through metamorphis to the next life stage but continues in the larval stage.
Paraphyletic
When a grouping of organisms for which known organisms outside the grouping also descended from the most recent common ancestor of the grouping, the grouping is called a paraphyletic group. In the taxonomy ‘Tree of Life’ (e.g. Class, Order, Family, Genus, Species, etc.), paraphyletic groups are avoided as much as possible. Contrast with monophyletic.
Phenotype
The observable expression of a genotype, influenced by natural selection or (in captivity) selective breeding. Please note that it is not always easy to determine if an observable characteristic is the result of the genotype or another factor, such as diet or climate.
Quantization
In Mathematics, when values from a large (often but not always infinite) set are mapped to a smaller subset of values, it is called quantization. For example, there are an infinite number of length value between 0 and 1 meter, but if we measure something to the nearest millimeter, we are are mapping that infinite set to a smaller finite set that contains 1000 possible values.
Quantization Error
Quantization Error is the difference between an actual value in a set and the quantized value it is assigned. Basically the rounding error.
Quantization Noise
Usually used in terms of audio and images, when an analog signal is quantized for digital storage, quantization noise is the difference between the analog signal that can be reproduced from that digital signal and the original band-limited analog signal before it was quantized. In audio, audible quantization noise has the characteristics of tape hiss. In audio recorded at 16 bits per sample at safe listening levels, quantization noise is only audible when the recording volume was too low. Increasing the bits per sample during recording gives more leeway with the recording volume, as the resulting quantization error is smaller. Professional equipment usually uses 32 bits per sample even though most professioal sound engineers are skilled enough to get by with just 16.
Shutterbug
A camera enthusiast who really enjoys pursuing high quality photography. Shutterbugs are very important to field guides and citizen science.
Speciation
The evolutionary process in which a population within a species differentiates itself from other populations within the species to become a distinct separate species from those other populations, on a different evolutionary path.
Species Complex
Two or more species that are so closely related they are difficult to distinguish from each other and may still have frequently occurring gene flow (introgression) happening.
Tetrapod
The monophyletic group including all vertebrates that possess four limbs, and all their descendants (such as snakes and legless lizards).
Ungulate
Hoofed mammals, such as deer and cattle and horses and pigs.
Vertebrate
Chordates that possess a backbone.

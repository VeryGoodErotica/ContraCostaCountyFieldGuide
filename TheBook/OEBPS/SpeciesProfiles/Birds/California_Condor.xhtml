California Condor (Gymnogyps californianus)

Species Overview

Stub. Content to be written.

The California Condor is one of the largest flying birds in the world.

In 1982, the California Condor was on the brink of extinction with only 23 of these magnificent scavengers left in the wild. The decision was made by the USFWS to capture all remaining condors and breed them in captivity to save the species, re-introducing them back into the wild when the population had grown.

The program thus far appears to be an incrfedible success story, with a total wild population of 312 birds and a captive population of 176 birds reported in the 2018 Annual Population Status Report.

Of those 312 wild birds, 188 were part of the California population.

Sightings of these birds in the San Francisco Bay Region are extremely rare, but they do on occassion happen.

Hopefully soon this bird will once again soar the skies above Contra Costa County.

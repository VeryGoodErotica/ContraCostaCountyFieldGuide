Similar Species

Both the Snowy Egret (Egretta thula) and the Cattle Egret are roughly the same shape and size with primarily white feathers. However the Cattle Egret has a yellowish to orange beak that readily distinguishes it from the Snowy Egret, which has a blueish gray beak, except for breeding adults which have a dark almost blackish beak.

Snowy Egrets and Cattle Egrets also have a different Natural History. Snowy Egrets largely feed upon fish and are thus far more likely to be spotted in or near water. Cattle Egrets feed largely on insects and are thus far more likely to be found in fields, especially in the vicinity of ungulates that both attract and root up insects.

The Great Egret (Ardea alba) is also similar in appearance to the Cattle Egret, though the Great Egret is a substantially larger bird.

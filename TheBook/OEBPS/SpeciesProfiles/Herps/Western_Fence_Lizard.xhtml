Range

Found throughout Contra Costa County at all elevations but are often scarce or absent in urban neighborhoods where outdoor domestic cats prey upon them.

Taxonomy Notes

First described in 1852 by Baird and Girard as Sceloporus occidentalis.

It appears that the Western Fence Lizard evolved from Eastern Fence Lizard Sceloporus undulatus.

Subspecies

The taxonomy of this species needs further genetic study.

With current subspecies based upon phenotype difference, the Western Fence Lizards in Contra Costa County are the Coast Range subspecies (S. o. bocourtii) with the possible excrption of the most extreme north-east corner of Contra Costa County, where they may be the Northwestern Fence Lizard (S. o. occidentalis).

The two subspecies are morphologically very close, they were considered conspecific in Stebbins 1985 though he did differentiate them in Stebbins 2003.

Morphological difference, which are what the current subspecies are defibes by, are often just expressions of localized natural selection pressures within the allele pool rather than distinct lineages.
